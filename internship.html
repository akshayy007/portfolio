BACKGROUND

Hyland Software is a leading content services provider. Hyland's OnBase Platform offers a suite of products that help its enterprise customers manage content, processes, and cases. We worked on one of its flagship products - Workflow.

What is OnBase Workflow?

The OnBase Workflow is an automation engine that enables organizations to perform business processes with speed and efficiency. It is primarily used by "administrators" or more technically-inclined users to build flows that route documents using a preconfigured set of rules and actions.
‍
These processes often follow standard set of business logic and are repeatable. Here is an example of a simple business process for routing vendor invoices.

What was the challenge?

The OnBase Workflow in its current state is offered via a dated and limiting desktop software client. For end-users, learning the interface, understanding terminology, and becoming proficient with the toolset requires significant training and trial. For customers, the training costs and knowledge transfer causes operational overhead.

As Hyland is shifting from desktop software to cloud-based, SaaS offerings, it needed a UX strategy for modernizing the Workflow that would

  • Stand up to top competitors in the market
  • Reduce the impediments to learning and use of the tool
  • Increase the adoption with existing and new customers

CONCEPT

In our proof of concept designs, we explored multiple solution ideas for a modernized SaaS Workflow product. We reimagined the UX of core components from the ground-up to produce concepts that focused on improving learnability and supporting the transition from old to new.

Visual approach for building complex flows

A simplified visual builder tool with a library of drag n drop components to build the flows intuitively.

Hybrid Tree View Builder for legacy users

An alternate tool that incorporates the legacy methods of building flows, offering familiar experience to the legacy users transitioning from old to a modern platform.

Collaboration space for Admins and Business Users

Business process designer tool to help organizations maintain a single source of truth for all the business processes. Admins can collaborate with Business Users to better understand the business logic and process change requests.

DESIGN PROCESS

Research Ideation Design Evaluation

We took a highly iterative approach for our design process that centered around research and explorations.

Research

Based on our initial project brief, we created a research plan to strategize our research. As there was no prior research specific to Workflow available, we needed to make sure that we prioritized the things that we wanted to learn.

Goals

  • Gain clarity on business objectives
  • Learn about the current state Workflow landscape
  • Analyze strategies adopted by competitors
  • Learn characteristics of the current target users and how they interact with the product

Methods

Stakeholder Interviews

Interviews with stakeholders in both group and one-on-one sessions
‍

User Interviews

In-depth interviews with 5 users of Workflow from Hyland’s Services team.
‍

Competitive Analysis

Reviewed the UX Research Team’s Competitive Analysis Report.
Some of the key competitors include Microsoft Power Automate, IBM, Appian, Nintex, Kissflow.
‍

What did we learn?

Our research uncovered numerous insights about user needs, business objectives, and competitive offerings. It was essential to identify overarching themes from the data as it established a robust set of guiding principles for the design ideation.

We identified the following four key focus areas from our research phase

Visual Approach

Transitioning to a more visual Workflow designer will improve ease of access and lower barriers to adoption.

Efficiency

The integration of feedback, analytics, and assistance components will encourage efficiency and make Workflow more competitive with other products that incorporate similar features.

Collaboration

Cross-functional, customizable, team-shared solutions will increase efficiency by permitting organization-wide collaboration and discussion, but only with distinct permissions settings.

Flexibility

Flexibility with integrating Hyland products, 3rd party apps, and repositories will expand Workflow’s robust capabilities and increase competitiveness.

Meet the target users

Primary

Bob the Admin

  • Has technical expertise to build, test and deploy workflow solutions
  • Gathers requirements from the business users
  • Relies heavily on knowledge of traditional OnBase terms and methods of building workflows

Secondary

Katie the Business User

  • Uses workflow solutions in her daily work as an HR rep.
  • Possesses departmental knowledge about how the business processes should work
  • Communicates process changes to the Admins

Ideation

With our set of guiding principles, we diverged to generate a multitude of high-level ideas for our design concept. Instead of focusing on incremental improvements, our goal was to reimagine the entire approach from the ground-up for the modern platform.

How did we prioritize the problem?

The research indicated that there were several challenges to conceptualizing a modernized Workflow product. A collaborative ideation workshop with the group of stakeholders uncovered the stakeholders' primary concern about modernization - the transition from old to a modern platform.

It made sense to keep the transition at the core of our concept because of the following reasons:

How might we ensure a smooth transition while improving the learnability of a modernized Workflow?

Exploring initial concepts

The key phases of the Workflow's UX journey are Planning, Building, Testing, and Deployment. We prioritized the Planning and Building phase as it involved the product's core functionalities and allowed us to set a realistic design scope as per the project timeline.

One of the design goals was to democratize the workflow building, which meant someone like Katie would be able to build the workflows themselves with minimal technical expertise.
‍
Concepts 2 and 3 illustrate the scenarios in which business users would be able to build the workflows. It raised an important question - Should the business users be given access to the build environment?
‍
After discussing the initial concepts with the stakeholders, we learned that

  • It may not be a practical approach as it could raise several concerns from a security and governance standpoint.
  • Business users' knowledge is often limited to their departmental processes, and their lack of system-level knowledge of interdependent processes could cause major breakdowns.

On the other hand, Concept 01

  • Addresses the security and governance concerns by keeping environments separate.
  • Leverages the business users' departmental expertise through collaboration module.

Hence we decided to explore the approach outlined in the concept 1.

Design

After multiple iterations of the wireframes, we converged on some key design decisions for our final proof of concept. While working on the high-fidelity prototype, I incorporated the design system components and patterns to align our designs more closely with the modern platform's design principles.‍‍

Supporting the transition for legacy users

  • Hybrid Tree View Builder
    A tool that incorporates the legacy methods of building flows, offering a familiar experience to the legacy users transitioning from old to a modern platform. The design conforms to the existing mental model of the legacy users.
  • An option to import legacy flows
    The customers migrating to the modern platform can use this option to import their existing flows into the new system.

Improving learnability through visual approach

  • Visual Builder Tool
    A visual method for building workflows using the library of drag n drop components. The design intended to simplify the workflow building for new Admin users without requiring knowledge of the legacy system.
  • Rethinking approach for terminologies
    Experimenting with a direct, simplified terminologies that reflect a more universal language.
  • Visual cues to boost recognition and mapping

Offering cross-functional collaboration space

  • Business Process Designer Tool/Workflow Playground
    A collaboration space for Business Users and Admins to map out the business processes. Sharing and commenting functionalities to support the collaboration.

Designing for efficiency and flexibility

  • Integration of other Hyland products and 3rd Party connections
    Interlinking products from Hyland's Admin Suite within the context of Workflow building to reduce the context-switching.
  • Change impact monitor
    Review mode to help Admins see the list of related flows impacted by the changes made to the current flow.
  • Pre-built templates and Reusable components
    Offering pre-built Workflow templates and the functionality to reuse the blocks of logic to save rework and time for the users.

Usability testing

The modern Workflow tool with the web interface is a major paradigm shift for the legacy users who have worked with the Desktop software for years. So we evaluated the core functionality of the concept by conducting usability testing sessions with 8 participants. Participants were from various verticals, internal and external, to Hyland.

Goals

  • Ensure that the updated Workflow platform matches users' mental models.
  • Ensure that the platform is intuitive.
  • Ensure that users can perform integral Workflow tasks, including creating, editing, and manipulating key Workflow functions.

Prototype used for testing

Based on our research questions we came up with the set of tasks for the usability testing. I refined the high-fidelity prototype and added interactions to make sure that the prototype supported these tasks effectively.

Key takeaways

  • Users' reactions to the prototype were overwhelmingly positive, and feedback consistently suggested that the modernized Workflow prototype was a big step in the right direction for an updated product. Users enjoyed the clean, simplified layout, organizational tagging and label features, and tools that helped reduce the burden on their memory.
  • Users indicated that the designs could better implement iconography and navigable text, as well as a more visible function for adding actions and timers to the solution.
  • Additional concerns included the ability to better navigate and search through complex logic flows; the implementation of learning aides, more collaborative resources, and control over screen real estate; and finally, a desire to be supported as they shift to the new product because of how drastically different these designs are from current state Workflow.
  • Insights unrelated to the prototype, but provided about Workflow, focused on improving the discovery process, implementing better management of user groups and permissions, additional documentation and resource concerns, suggestions for testing and for test licenses, and document navigation and manipulation.

IMPACT

Unified vision
When we started this project, the stakeholders communicated different visions of the modernized Workflow product. However, through our proof of concept, we established a unified vision that strategically aligned with the business objectives and kept the end-users at the center.

Big win for UX
‍The part of our job was to champion the UX process while interacting with stakeholders from different backgrounds and teams. The conversations around our work convinced stakeholders that we needed to take the UX first approach for the new product, which was a big win for us.

How would we measure success?

  • Reduced training costs and less calls for support
  • Increased number of new subscriptions and rate of upgrades by the legacy customers
  • Increased NPS for Hyland and Hyland's customers using modern Workflow solutions

LEARNINGS

The more you involve the stakeholders in the design process, the more they are likely to support the design decisions.
‍
It is essential to be comfortable with ambiguity and keep making progress. It helps open up new paths for learning.
‍
Striking a balance between pragmatic solutions and the blue sky vision can be challenging, and it's imperative to know when to push the boundaries for innovative thinking.

What would I do differently?

  • I would involve more low-fidelity and storyboarding to communicate concepts early in the process.
  • I would involve the end users early on in the design process either by conducting collaborative ideation session or getting their feedback on a high-level concept.
